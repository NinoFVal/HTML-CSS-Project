Hatha Yoga

Hatha Yoga is one of the most traditional and widely practised forms of yoga. The word "hatha" is derived from two Sanskrit words: "ha," which means sun, and "that," which means moon. It represents the union of opposites, balancing the masculine and feminine energies within the body.

Hatha Yoga primarily focuses on physical postures (asanas) and breath control (pranayama) to align, strengthen, and purify the body and mind. It is an excellent starting point for beginners because it offers a foundation for other yoga styles and provides a gentle introduction to yoga practice.

Hatha Yoga is not only beneficial for physical health but also for mental and emotional well-being. Regular practice can lead to increased flexibility, strength, and balance, as well as reduced stress and anxiety.


Vinyasa Yoga

Vinyasa Yoga is a dynamic and flowing style of yoga that emphasizes the coordination of breath with movement. The word "Vinyasa" comes from the Sanskrit words "vi," which means variation, and "nyasa," which means within prescribed parameters.

In Vinyasa Yoga, practitioners move through a series of poses in a smooth and continuous sequence, with each movement linked to the breath.

Vinyasa Yoga classes can range from gentle and relaxing to vigorous and physically demanding, depending on the instructor's style and the level of the class. It is a popular choice for those who enjoy movement, variety, and a more energetic approach to yoga.

Whether you're looking to build strength, increase flexibility, reduce stress, or simply enjoy the flow of movement, Vinyasa Yoga offers a dynamic and transformative practice suitable for practitioners of all levels.


Ashtanga Yoga

Ashtanga is a vigorous and structured form of yoga that follows a specific sequence of postures. It involves synchronized breathing, and practitioners progress through the series at their own pace.


Tristhana

Ashtanga Yoga emphasizes three focal points during practice known as Tristhana.


Breath (Pranayama)
Ujjayi breath, a deep and audible breath, is used throughout the practice to create internal heat and aid concentration.


Posture (Asana)
Ashtanga follows a specific sequence of postures, which are organized into six series.The primary series (Yoga Chikitsa) is the first series and focuses on building a strong foundation. The practice then progresses to more challenging series as the practitioner becomes more proficient.


Gaze (Drishti)
Each asana has a specific gazing point (Drishti), which helps to focus the mind and improve concentration during the practice.


Yin Yoga

Yin Yoga is a slow-paced and gentle style of yoga that targets the deep connective tissues of the body, such as ligaments, tendons, and fascia. Unlike more dynamic and active styles of yoga.

Yin Yoga involves holding passive postures for an extended period, typically ranging from 3 to 5 minutes or even longer. The extended holds allow the muscles to relax, enabling the practitioner to access deeper layers of the body.

Yin Yoga is not about achieving extreme flexibility or pushing the body to its limits. Instead, it encourages practitioners to explore their physical and emotional edges with a sense of acceptance and patience. The practice can lead to increased flexibility, improved joint health, and a sense of inner peace.

Because Yin Yoga focuses on stillness and holding poses for an extended time, it can be a deeply restorative and calming practice. It is often recommended as a complement to more active forms of yoga or other physical activities, providing balance and harmony to the body and mind.


Power Yoga

Power yoga is a dynamic and physically challenging style that is based on the Ashtanga tradition but without a strict set sequence. It often includes strength-building exercises and vigorous movements.

Power Yoga is a dynamic and vigorous style of yoga that draws inspiration from Ashtanga Yoga, but it doesn't strictly adhere to a fixed sequence of poses. Power Yoga is characterized by its focus on strength-building, intensity, and fluid movements, making it an excellent choice for individuals seeking a challenging and physically demanding practice.

Power Yoga is a challenging and empowering practice that can help practitioners develop physical strength, flexibility, and mental focus. It can also be an effective way to manage stress and cultivate a sense of presence and mindfulness.
